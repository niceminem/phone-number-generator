About US Phone Numbers

US phone numbers follow the North American Numbering Plan (NANP) format: (NXX) NXX-XXXX, where N is a digit from 2-9 and X is a digit from 0-9. The first three digits represent the area code, which identifies the geographic region.

Key features of US phone numbers:

  • Area Codes: Three-digit codes that identify geographic regions
  • Exchange Codes: The first three digits after the area code
  • Subscriber Number: The last four digits that identify the specific line
  • Toll-Free Numbers: Begin with 800, 888, 877, 866, 855, 844, or 833
